In A Search Refinement,
A Chance To Rival Google

Powerset and PARC Signed Exclusive Deal to Commercialize Breakthrough Search Engine Technology in Consumer Search.

PARC announced a deal that underscores its recent strategy. It is licensing a broad portfolio of patents and technology to a well-financed start-up [Powerset] with an ambitious and potentially lucrative goal: to build a search engine that could some day rival Google.

In Silicon Valley and beyond, PARC has often been called the lab of missed opportunities. It has been credited with many breakthroughs, including the graphical user interface and the Ethernet networking technology, that have revolutionized the computer industry, but that were commercialized by others.

"There's no way anyone can top what they did in the past in terms of dramatic research developments," said the futurist Paul Saffo, a fellow at the Institute for the Future. But Mr. Saffo praised PARC for finding a business model that has allowed it to survive at a time when many research groups at American corporations are being cut.

Sick and Tired of
 Emoticons doing nothing ?

Am I a cute Moji?

 (One of the Moji pet breeds. Mor(f) Dynamics is still coming up with character designs that will appeal to all. )

According to Mor(f) CEO Robert Fong, there are three parts to the Moji system.

The first is the 3D engine, which is used to animate the pet and enable it to express a range of emotions. "We're sick and tired of emoticons doing nothing – we wanted to see something that provides a more interactive and engaging experience, which is why we came up with the idea of 3D pets," Fong explained.

The second and third components involve the highly complex areas of artificial intelligence and computational linguistics.

In basic terms, these components enable the pet to "come alive" by allowing it to learn from the user and react accordingly in real-time.

It does so by using natural language processing techniques to analyse or parse text in a user's speech at a semantic level, Fong said.
